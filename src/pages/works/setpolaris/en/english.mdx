## What is SetPolaris

SetPolaris is a small companion app for setting up a telescope at night. It shows you true north (not magnetic north) and the device's level — on a single dark-sky-friendly screen — so you can polar-align your equatorial mount quickly in the dark.

Aligning a mount needs **true north**, not magnetic north. At the same time, the tripod has to sit **level**. SetPolaris brings both readouts together so you can finish the setup with fewer back-and-forth glances.

## Features

### True north
Combines the device's gyroscope and magnetometer with your latitude and longitude to correct for magnetic declination. Stripped down to what telescope setup actually needs — no map widgets, no clutter.

### Level indicator
Real-time tilt readout. Place the device on the mount head while extending the tripod legs and you'll know the moment it sits flat.

### Dark-sky friendly UI
Black background with red text. Bright screens contract your pupils and cost you the dark adaptation that lets you see faint stars. SetPolaris uses the same red-light convention astronomers have followed for decades.

### Works offline
No network required. Your location is used only on-device to compute magnetic declination — it never leaves your phone.

## Who it's for

- Telescope users observing or imaging nebulae, clusters, and planets
- Anyone who wants polar alignment to be less of a chore
- People who don't want a bright screen blasting their night vision
- Campers, hikers, and other outdoor folks who'd rather keep things dim

## How to use

1. Open SetPolaris outdoors with a clear view of the sky.
2. Allow location access if prompted — your latitude and longitude are used only to compute true north (nothing is sent off-device).
3. Place the device on a level surface or mount head and align your telescope to the displayed bearing.

## Requirements

- iOS 15.6+ / iPadOS 15.6+
- Location access (used to compute true north)
- iPhone and iPad supported
